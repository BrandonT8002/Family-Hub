FAMILYHUB — WISHLIST SYSTEM (Full Breakdown)

Core Purpose

Wishlists are not just “wants.”

They are:

• A communication tool
• A budgeting awareness tool
• A gifting coordination system
• A family transparency tool
• A boundary-respecting alternative to awkward conversations

They allow users to express what they want without pressure.

They reduce:
• Guessing during holidays
• Duplicate gifts
• Overspending
• Emotional misalignment

⸻

1. Wishlist Types

Users can create multiple wishlists.

Wishlists are not limited to one per person.

Examples:

• Birthday List
• Christmas List
• “Someday” List
• Clothing Needs
• Upgrade List
• Home Improvements
• Baby Registry
• Graduation List
• Dream Board

Users name their own lists.

No preset categories required.

⸻

2. Wants vs Needs Separation

Each list item can be marked as:

• Want
• Need

This helps families:

• Understand urgency
• Budget properly
• Avoid impulse purchases
• See patterns in spending

Children 12 and under:
• Cannot publicly share lists outside family without permission

13+:
• Can manage own lists with visibility controls

⸻

3. Visibility Controls

Each wishlist can be:

• Private
• Shared with family
• Shared with specific members
• Shared with connected outside family

Owner controls visibility rules for minors.

Adults control their own visibility fully.

No cross-family automatic access.

⸻

4. Item Details Per Wishlist Entry

Each item can include:

• Item name
• Category (clothing, tech, books, etc.)
• Estimated price
• Store name
• Optional store link (manual entry only)
• Notes (size, color, version, preference)
• Priority level (Low / Medium / High)
• Want or Need tag
• Status (Unclaimed / Claimed / Purchased)

Optional:
• Photo attachment

No scraping required.
All user-controlled input.

⸻

5. Claim System (Gift Coordination)

For shared wishlists:

Family members can:

• Mark item as “Claimed”
• Mark item as “Purchased”
• Leave private note (“ordered already”)

Important:
If claimed, the person who owns the list cannot see who claimed it (optional setting).

This keeps surprises intact.

Owner can choose:
• Show who claimed
• Hide who claimed

⸻

6. Outside Family Integration

If two families connect:

They can:

• Share specific wishlists
• Coordinate gifts
• Avoid duplicate purchases
• Schedule exchanges

They cannot:

• See finances
• See goals
• See private diaries
• See full schedule

Wishlist sharing is permission-based.

⸻

7. Budget Awareness Integration

Wishlists integrate with Money tab.

Users can:

• See total estimated wishlist cost
• Compare wishlist vs savings goal
• Track how much was spent vs planned

This helps families:

• Avoid holiday debt
• Plan months in advance
• Spread purchases over time

⸻

8. Long-Term Wishlist Mode

Users can create:

• Dream list (long-term)
• 5-year goal wishlist
• “When money is right” list

These can connect to long-term savings goals.

Example:
“Vacation to Hawaii”
• Linked to $4,000 savings goal

⸻

9. Seasonal Mode

Wishlists can be:

• Time-based (Holiday mode)
• Open-ended
• Year-round

Polls can be attached:
“What do we prioritize first?”

⸻

10. Shopping List Integration

Wishlist items can:

• Be moved to shopping list
• Be converted to goal
• Be converted to money-linked savings target

Example:
Wishlist item → Move to “Needs List” → Add to grocery run.

⸻

11. Duplicate Protection

System prevents:

• Same person claiming twice
• Duplicate purchase markers
• Accidental double claims

Soft warning only.

⸻

12. Emotional Purpose

Wishlists reduce tension by:

• Removing awkward asking
• Giving clarity
• Allowing children to express interest
• Giving adults transparency
• Making gift-giving strategic

It becomes a communication bridge.

⸻

13. Privacy Summary

Adults:
• Full control

Teens:
• Personal control unless owner restricts

Children:
• Owner-controlled visibility

Outside families:
• Access only to explicitly shared lists

No one sees:
• Private lists without permission
• Financial information tied to list unless allowed

⸻

14. Retention Factor

Wishlists encourage:

• Year-round engagement
• Seasonal engagement spikes
• Cross-family interaction
• Financial planning

If removed, families would feel:
• Less organized during holidays
• More last-minute stress
• More duplicate purchases
