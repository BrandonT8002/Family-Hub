Overview

The Schedule system is the operational backbone of the family.

It is designed to:
	•	Reduce forgotten events
	•	Separate personal and shared life
	•	Prevent oversharing
	•	Support recurring responsibilities
	•	Encourage accountability
	•	Respect privacy
	•	Integrate gently into daily routines

It is not just a calendar.
It is a structured family coordination system.

⸻

Core Philosophy

The schedule should:
	•	Feel calm, not chaotic
	•	Feel intentional, not noisy
	•	Respect personal boundaries
	•	Encourage responsibility without pressure
	•	Support both older adults and teenagers
	•	Be flexible but structured

The schedule is a coordination tool — not a surveillance tool.

⸻

Schedule Architecture

1. Event Types

Every event can be:
	•	Personal (visible only to the creator)
	•	Shared with Family
	•	Shared with specific members (future phase)
	•	Financial (lives in Money tab instead)

Users decide visibility at creation.

Privacy is default-respecting.

⸻

2. Event Creation

When adding an event, users input:
	•	Title
	•	Date
	•	Time
	•	Recurrence type
	•	Visibility (personal or shared)

Optional:
	•	Notes
	•	Reminder timing (future phase)
	•	Pre-leave checklist trigger
	•	Location (future phase)

The “Add Event” flow is simple and clean.

⸻

3. Recurrence System

Supported recurrence options:
	•	One time
	•	Daily
	•	Weekly
	•	Monthly
	•	Yearly

Examples:
	•	Dentist (one time)
	•	Payday (weekly)
	•	Rent (monthly)
	•	Anniversary (yearly)
	•	Workout reminder (daily)

Recurring events generate future instances automatically.

⸻

4. Calendar Format (Planned Format)

The schedule supports:
	•	Month view
	•	Week view
	•	Day view (future phase)
	•	Agenda list below calendar

Users can:
	•	Tap a day to view events
	•	Switch between month and week
	•	Scroll through future months

Days with events display indicator dots.

⸻

5. Personal vs Family Separation

Personal events:
	•	Do not appear on family schedule
	•	Are visible only to creator
	•	Allow teen autonomy

Family events:
	•	Appear for all members
	•	Can include recitals, school events, travel, holidays

This separation prevents:
	•	Oversharing
	•	Unnecessary drama
	•	Teen privacy conflict

⸻

6. Financial Schedule Separation

Important financial events:
	•	Rent
	•	Netflix
	•	Insurance
	•	Payday

Do NOT clutter the family calendar.

They live inside the Money tab.

They are tracked separately but can optionally appear as reminders.

⸻

7. Morning “Walk Out” Feature

Optional feature:

When user opens the app in the morning:

Instead of:
“What time are you leaving?”

It asks softly:

“What time are you planning on walking out today?”

If set:
	•	Triggers a pre-leave checklist
	•	Optional reminder shortly before leaving
	•	Encourages punctuality without alarm-clock aggression

Checklist examples:
	•	Keys
	•	Wallet
	•	ID
	•	Insurance card (for doctor visit)
	•	School bag

This feature is optional.

⸻

8. Gentle Reminder System

Notifications are:
	•	Respectful
	•	Functional
	•	Not addictive
	•	Not spammy

No “Come back to app” nudges.

Only event-related reminders.

Users can:
	•	Choose notification style
	•	Enable stronger alerts if needed
	•	Disable if preferred

⸻

9. Ownership & Permissions

Owner controls:
	•	Who can create shared events
	•	Who can delete events
	•	Who can see family schedule
	•	Under-18 sharing permissions

12 and under:
	•	No independent email required
	•	Parent-controlled
	•	Limited schedule permissions

13–14:
	•	Limited independence
	•	Email integration allowed with approval

15–17:
	•	Near-adult permissions
	•	Personal events respected

Adults:
	•	Full personal privacy
	•	Owner cannot read private schedules unless shared

⸻

10. Caregiver Mode (Limited Access)

Optional caregiver:
	•	Can see assigned schedule items
	•	Cannot view private personal events
	•	Cannot view financial data
	•	Cannot see diary entries

Caregivers are limited in number.

⸻

11. Conflict Protection

If someone blocks another:
	•	24-hour cooldown before unblocking
	•	Extra confirmation prompt before blocking

Schedule access is not automatically removed by blocking chat.

⸻

12. Event Notes

Each event can include notes.

Notes can be:
	•	Visible to everyone
	•	Visible to creator only
	•	Visible to specific people (future phase)

Notes help clarify:
	•	Bring insurance card
	•	Dress rehearsal outfit required
	•	Arrive 15 minutes early

⸻

13. Shared View Sync (Parent + Student)

Academic events:
	•	Can be built by parent
	•	Can be built by student
	•	Can be shared

Students can:
	•	Build independent schedule
	•	Choose what to share

Parents can:
	•	Monitor academics without micromanaging

⸻

14. Yearly Snapshots

Over time, schedule history can support:
	•	Annual review
	•	Event frequency tracking
	•	Habit consistency
	•	Milestone recognition

Example:
	•	“You attended 42 workouts this year.”
	•	“You saved 10 recurring late arrivals.”

⸻

15. Emotional Design

The Schedule tab should feel:
	•	Clean
	•	Organized
	•	Mature
	•	Empowering
	•	Respectful

Not:
	•	Cluttered
	•	Loud
	•	Controlling
	•	Chaotic
