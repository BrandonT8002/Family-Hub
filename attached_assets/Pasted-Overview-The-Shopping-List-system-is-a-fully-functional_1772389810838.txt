Overview

The Shopping List system is a fully functional, intelligent list builder designed to reduce grocery stress, prevent overspending, and streamline in-store shopping.

It is more than a checklist.

It combines:
	•	Categorization by aisle
	•	Price tracking
	•	Store comparison
	•	Budget awareness
	•	Wants vs Needs separation
	•	Multi-store coordination

The goal is efficiency + financial clarity.

⸻

Core Structure

1. Multiple Lists

Users can create unlimited lists:

Examples:
	•	Weekly Groceries
	•	Costco Run
	•	School Supplies
	•	Christmas Gifts
	•	Hardware Store
	•	Vacation Packing List

Each list can be:
	•	Personal
	•	Shared with selected family members
	•	Visible to outside approved families (optional)

⸻

2. Grocery Aisle Organization

When creating a grocery list, items are automatically categorized into store sections.

Default aisle categories:
	•	Produce
	•	Meat
	•	Seafood
	•	Dairy
	•	Frozen
	•	Bakery
	•	Deli
	•	Pantry / Dry Goods
	•	Beverages
	•	Snacks
	•	Household
	•	Toiletries
	•	Baby
	•	Pet
	•	Pharmacy
	•	Miscellaneous

Users can:
	•	Reassign items to different categories
	•	Create custom aisle categories
	•	Hide unused categories

Items are displayed grouped by aisle to match how users move through the store.

Example view:

Produce
	•	Apples
	•	Bananas

Meat
	•	Ground Beef
	•	Chicken Breast

Dairy
	•	Milk
	•	Cheese

This reduces backtracking in the store.

⸻

3. Smart Categorization Prompt

If a user adds an item without selecting a category:

The app asks:

“Is this a produce item, dairy, meat, or something else?”

This keeps the list organized automatically.

⸻

4. Multi-Store Support

Users can assign a store to a list.

Features:
	•	Select from saved stores
	•	Add new store manually
	•	Multiple stores for one trip
	•	Store-specific pricing memory

Example:
	•	Walmart list
	•	Publix list
	•	Costco list

⸻

5. Item-Level Price Tracking

Users can manually enter item prices:

Example:
	•	Ground beef – $2.14/lb at Walmart

Next visit:
	•	Ground beef – $2.16/lb

The system tracks:
	•	Price changes over time
	•	Store comparison averages
	•	Inflation trends

This data feeds into the Money tab for weekly/monthly spending reports.

Price tracking is optional.

Users can skip pricing entirely.

⸻

6. Check-Off System

While in the store:
	•	Tap item → mark as picked up
	•	Completed items move to bottom or fade visually
	•	Optional “remove checked items after checkout”

Designed for one-handed use while shopping.

⸻

7. Wants vs Needs

Users can create separate lists:

Needs:
	•	Essentials
	•	Groceries
	•	Household supplies

Wants:
	•	Gift ideas
	•	Personal shopping
	•	Clothing
	•	Tech

Visibility controls:
	•	13+ users can control who sees their list
	•	12 and under require owner permissions

⸻

8. Budget Awareness

Each list shows:
	•	Estimated total (if prices entered)
	•	Tax estimate (optional)
	•	Running total as items are checked off

Optional grocery calculator:
	•	Adds tax
	•	Updates live as prices entered
	•	Shows difference from previous week

⸻

9. Weekly & Monthly Snapshots

Shopping data feeds into:
	•	Weekly grocery total
	•	Monthly grocery comparison
	•	Store average comparison
	•	Year-over-year grocery cost trends

This ties directly into financial awareness.

⸻

10. Coordination Mode

Multiple users can:
	•	Add items
	•	Remove items (if permitted)
	•	Assign items to someone
	•	Leave notes on items

Example:
“Don’t forget organic.”
“Only if on sale.”

⸻

Permission Model

Owner controls:
	•	Who can delete items
	•	Who can edit pricing
	•	Who can see wants lists
	•	Who can share outside the family

13–14 users:
	•	Limited delete permissions (owner decides)

15+:
	•	More autonomy

⸻

Design Philosophy

The shopping list should:
	•	Feel fast
	•	Feel practical
	•	Reduce store anxiety
	•	Reduce forgotten items
	•	Reduce impulse buying
	•	Support financial awareness

It should feel like a grocery companion, not a spreadsheet.

⸻

Emotional Goal

If this feature disappeared, users would feel:
	•	Disorganized in stores
	•	More forgetful
	•	Less aware of price increases
	•	Less in control of grocery spending

This feature supports:
	•	Efficiency
	•	Financial literacy
	•	Shared household responsibility
