Overview

The Money Management tab is a private financial awareness and savings system designed to help families understand spending patterns, reduce waste, and build long-term wealth.

It is not a banking app.
It does not connect directly to bank accounts.
It focuses on intentional manual tracking to increase awareness and accountability.

The goal is clarity, not complexity.

⸻

Core Sections

1. Financial Dashboard (Overview)

The top of the tab shows:
	•	Total spent this week
	•	Total spent this month
	•	Category breakdown (pie chart)
	•	Comparison to previous week/month
	•	Active savings goals progress

This section answers:
	•	Where is our money going?
	•	Are we spending more than usual?
	•	Are we improving?

⸻

2. Expense Tracking

Users can manually log expenses.

Each expense includes:
	•	Amount
	•	Category (customizable)
	•	Store/vendor
	•	Date
	•	Optional notes
	•	Optional tag (grocery, subscription, gas, etc.)

Expenses can be:
	•	Personal
	•	Shared family

Children can view financials only if the owner allows.

⸻

3. Category System

Categories are customizable by the owner.

Examples:
	•	Groceries
	•	Rent
	•	Utilities
	•	Subscriptions
	•	Gas
	•	Clothing
	•	Dining Out
	•	Entertainment
	•	School
	•	Miscellaneous

Users can add new categories at any time.

⸻

4. Financial Schedule (Separate from Family Calendar)

This section tracks recurring financial obligations:
	•	Rent / mortgage
	•	Netflix / streaming
	•	Insurance
	•	Payday
	•	Utility due dates

Each item can be:
	•	One-time
	•	Recurring (weekly, monthly, yearly)

These items do NOT appear in the family event calendar.
They live inside the money tab to avoid clutter.

⸻

5. Grocery & Shopping Analytics

Integrated with the shopping list system.

Features:
	•	Manual price entry per item
	•	Track price changes per store
	•	Compare store averages
	•	Weekly and monthly grocery spend snapshot
	•	Yearly grocery comparison

Purpose:
	•	Provide evidence of inflation impact
	•	Identify price increases
	•	Encourage smarter store decisions

Users can:
	•	Skip price tracking entirely
	•	Use a simplified “total spent only” mode

⸻

6. Savings Goals

Users can create money-linked goals:

Examples:
	•	$1,500 couch fund
	•	Vacation fund
	•	Emergency savings
	•	School supplies budget

Each goal includes:
	•	Target amount
	•	Current saved amount
	•	Visual progress indicator
	•	Optional family participation

Progress can be:
	•	Manual contributions
	•	Linked to expense reduction

⸻

7. Financial Snapshots

Automatic summaries:
	•	Weekly spending snapshot
	•	Monthly comparison
	•	Year-over-year comparison
	•	Grocery trend analysis
	•	Category growth or reduction

The goal is pattern awareness over time.

⸻

Privacy & Permissions
	•	Financial data is private by default.
	•	Owner controls who can view financials.
	•	Teens (15–17) can have controlled access.
	•	Adults have independent privacy.

No financial data is shared outside the family.
No ads.
No third-party tracking.

⸻

Design Philosophy

The Money tab should feel:
	•	Clean
	•	Calm
	•	Honest
	•	Insightful
	•	Non-judgmental

It should never shame users.
It should encourage improvement through visibility.

⸻

Emotional Goal

This tab should:
	•	Help families save money
	•	Reduce stress about spending
	•	Encourage financial conversations
	•	Show long-term improvement
	•	Create transparency without control

If removed, families would lose:
	•	Spending awareness
	•	Goal clarity
	•	Grocery price tracking
	•	Financial structure
